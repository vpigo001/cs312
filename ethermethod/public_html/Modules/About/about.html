About

Super Smash Bros.: Melee is a fighting-style video game for the Nintendo Gamecube released on November 21, 2001. Originally intended to be a party game, Melee was quickly taken toward a competitive direction by its players. Hundreds of advanced tactics have been discovered over the years, and the competition is faster, stronger, and more ruthless than ever. For more information on the history of the game and its players, please see the Super Smash Bros.: Melee documentary created by Samox here.

Vincent "Ether" Pigott is a Marth player from the Southern Virginia region. He started playing competitively in 2005 and is one of the few Marth players to not struggle with the Sheik matchup. Ether has won many local tournaments and has placed high at major events, including a top 32 finish at Apex and a 9th place finish at Revival of Melee. Ether has helped grow the melee community in SoVa for years and is currently on a hiatus while he works to finish his degree in Computer Science.
